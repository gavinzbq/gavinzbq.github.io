Baidu's Robin and Alibaba's Jack

It baffled me when people introduce themselves as "call me Tom / Jerry / Max" given they come from the same culture as mine. I think our given names are beautiful and English names are not so cool. However, things are there for a reason. English names do serve as an approach for an organization (especially a tech company) achieve "flat" management.

We do not just learn the syntax when learning a language, we learn the way its speakers think and communicate -- the culture. Since human rights equality and freedom are norms in America, addressing other people by given names is everyday life, no matter they are younger / older / peers, your relatives or not, share one academic advisor, your co-workers or boss. In circumstances of lacking hierarchy, more effective communications and more mutual respects are expected. Company executives want their engineers switch to this cultural mind-set.

An English name is also useful when trying to order a coffee. People from different cultures give their English nicknames because they are nice and want to make your life easier.

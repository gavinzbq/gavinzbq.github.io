Baidu's Robin and Alibaba's Jack

It baffled me when people introduce themselves as "call me Tom / Jerry / Max" given they come from the same culture as mine. I especially dispised those who, work in foreign company based in China, and let other people call their English nicknames. "What a shame they lose pride in our culture and worship western countires for no reason.", I said to myself.

I became a little bit rational lately.

We do not just learn the language when learning English, we learn the way English speakers think, react and communicate -- the culture behind the language. In such a context, addressing other people by given names is everyday life, no matter they are younger / older / peers, your relatives or not, share one academic advisor with you or not, your co-workers or boss. It is not required or even not expected to call people by their positions. The purpose, of course, is that a lack of hierarchy will encourage the idea that all livings being treated equally. It is one of the most important values America is founded.

Coming back to the foreign companies and tech companies in China, using English names indicate workers switch to a different cultural mind-set. CEOs express respect to their engineers by demonstrating you are working in a horizontal structure and not a hierarchical one, therefore feel free to follow and share your own ideas among us.

For now I do not use a English name and I give my last name when order a Starbuck. I love my given name and I think my parents are brilliant.

Bear in mind my English speaker friends, people from different nations allow you call their English names because they are nice and they want to make your life easier.

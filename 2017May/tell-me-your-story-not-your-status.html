Tell me your story, not your status

Original article: Tell Me Your Story, Not Your Status.

I agree with the saying, "A status is a static snapshot of a handful of labels attached to a person based on some institutions or external standards. It conveys nothing really unique that gets to the core of the person, or the animating force behind their actions and ideas. There is no passion in it. No sense of direction and creativity."

One of the main reasons I built this website is the disbelief of resume representing me as a human being. I would be glad if you are impressed by my knowledge, skills or previous experience, and not so happy if turned down because university I attended / previous organizations where I worked are not sufficiently prestigious.

For me, I can talk about how the greatest accomplishment was achieved in my research, how I started to learn programming, what is it like to keep playing basketball and piano for over twenty years, my opinion on "expert generalist" and so on. Stories and mind make a person lively and unique.

Here is a great article illustrating how to tell our stories effectively in most scenarios.

How to Talk About Yourself in an Interview.

Share stories, do not judge on status so quickly.
